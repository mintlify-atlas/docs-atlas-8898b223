The AI Story Generation feature uses Google Gemini AI to create imaginative retellings of biblical narratives. Generate stories with custom perspectives, settings, tones, and lengths while maintaining theological integrity.

<Warning>
  AI Story Generation is a premium feature that requires you to be signed in. Stories are generated using AI and subject to rate limiting.
</Warning>

## Getting Started

Create your first AI-generated story:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign In">
    Ensure you're signed in to your account. Story generation is only available to authenticated users.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Navigate to Stories">
    Go to the Stories section from the main navigation menu.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create Story">
    Click the "Create Story" button to begin the story generation process.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Bible Passage">
    Choose:
    - **Bible translation**: Which version to base the story on
    - **Chapter**: The specific chapter you want to reimagine
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure Parameters">
    Customize your story using the available parameters (detailed below).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Generate">
    Click "Generate Story" and wait while the AI creates your narrative.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Story generation typically takes 10-30 seconds depending on the length and complexity of the passage.
</Info>

## Customization Parameters

Control how your story is generated with five key parameters:

### Title

Give your story a meaningful title:

- **Purpose**: Helps you identify and organize your stories
- **Requirement**: Must be provided (cannot be empty)
- **Tips**: Use descriptive titles like "The Good Samaritan from the Priest's View" or "Creation Week in Modern Times"

### Perspective

Choose the narrative point of view:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="First Person">
    **Tell the story as a participant:**
    
    - "I walked along the road to Jericho..."
    - Experience events through a character's eyes
    - Deeply personal and immersive
    - Examples: "As Peter, I witnessed...", "I was the woman at the well..."
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Third Person (Observer)">
    **Narrate from outside the story:**
    
    - "He walked along the road to Jericho..."
    - Traditional storytelling perspective
    - Can describe multiple viewpoints
    - More objective narrative style
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Custom Perspectives">
    **Get creative:**
    
    - Minor character: "As a shepherd that night..."
    - Inanimate object: "From the perspective of the stone..."
    - Omniscient narrator: "Knowing all hearts and minds..."
    - Contemporary observer: "As a modern journalist covering..."
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Card title="Perspective Examples" icon="eye">
  - **"observer"**: Traditional third-person narration
  - **"Mary, mother of Jesus"**: First-person from Mary's viewpoint
  - **"a Roman soldier"**: Experience events from a soldier's perspective
  - **"an angel"**: Celestial viewpoint on earthly events
</Card>

### Setting

Describe the context or environment for your story:

**Purpose**: Reimagine the biblical narrative in different contexts

**Examples:**

- **Historical accuracy**: "First century Judea, traditional setting"
- **Different time period**: "Medieval Europe", "Ancient China", "Victorian England"
- **Modern setting**: "Contemporary New York City", "Rural farming community"
- **Fictional worlds**: "Space station", "Fantasy kingdom", "Post-apocalyptic wasteland"
- **Alternative history**: "What if it happened in..."

<Note>
  While the setting can be creative, the AI maintains the core theological message and divine actions from the original passage.
</Note>

### Tone

Set the emotional or thematic tone:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Reflective">
    **Contemplative and thoughtful:**
    
    - Deep meditation on themes
    - Slower pacing
    - Emphasis on meaning and significance
    - Good for: Psalms, wisdom literature, teaching passages
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Adventurous">
    **Action-oriented and exciting:**
    
    - Dynamic and fast-paced
    - Emphasizes action and drama
    - Engaging narrative flow
    - Good for: Journey narratives, battles, escapes
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Hopeful">
    **Uplifting and encouraging:**
    
    - Focus on redemption and restoration
    - Positive outlook
    - Emphasis on God's goodness
    - Good for: Redemption stories, promises, victories
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Mysterious">
    **Intriguing and thought-provoking:**
    
    - Elements of wonder and mystery
    - Questions and discovery
    - Emphasis on the unknown
    - Good for: Prophecies, visions, miracles
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

**More tone options:**

- **Inspirational**: Motivating and uplifting
- **Solemn**: Serious and reverent
- **Joyful**: Celebratory and exuberant
- **Dramatic**: High tension and emotion

### Story Length

Control the length of your generated story:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Short">
    **~200 words**
    
    - Quick read (1-2 minutes)
    - Focuses on key events
    - Best for simple narratives
    - Faster generation time
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Medium">
    **~500 words**
    
    - Balanced length (3-5 minutes)
    - Includes more detail and context
    - Good for most passages
    - Recommended default option
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Long">
    **~1000 words**
    
    - Comprehensive retelling (7-10 minutes)
    - Rich detail and character development
    - Best for complex narratives
    - Longer generation time
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Story length is approximate. The AI may adjust slightly based on natural story flow and chapter content.
</Info>

## Theological Guidelines

The AI is programmed with strict theological and ethical guidelines:

### Core Principles

Every generated story adheres to these rules:

1. **Doctrinal Integrity**: Core biblical doctrines are never contradicted
2. **Reverence**: God and biblical figures are treated with respect
3. **Narrative Consistency**: Essential plot points and divine actions are preserved
4. **Appropriate Content**: No explicit violence, sexual content, or inappropriate material
5. **Creative Boundaries**: Setting and perspective can change, but theological truth remains

<Card title="What the AI Won't Do" icon="shield">
  - Contradict core biblical teachings
  - Disrespect God or biblical figures  
  - Invent new theological doctrines
  - Add significant events that change the narrative's meaning
  - Create inappropriate or offensive content
  - Replace divine intervention with natural explanations
</Card>

### What Makes a Good AI Story

The best AI stories:

- **Respect the original**: Core message and events are preserved
- **Add creativity**: New perspectives and settings bring fresh insights
- **Remain reverent**: Maintains appropriate tone for Scripture
- **Engage the reader**: Well-written and compelling narrative
- **Provoke thought**: Helps you see familiar passages in new ways

## Rate Limiting

To manage API costs and ensure fair usage, story generation is rate limited:

### How Rate Limiting Works

- **Per-user limits**: Limits are applied to your user account
- **Time-based windows**: Limits reset after a specific time period
- **Redis-powered**: Uses Redis for fast, accurate rate limit tracking

### When You Hit the Limit

If you exceed your rate limit:

1. **Error message**: You'll see a clear error explaining the limit
2. **Remaining requests**: The message shows how many requests remain
3. **Wait period**: Information about when you can generate again
4. **Typical message**: "Rate limit exceeded. Try again in a few hours. Remaining requests: 0."

<Warning>
  Rate limits are in place to control costs associated with the Google Gemini API. Be thoughtful about story generation and create stories you're genuinely interested in.
</Warning>

<Info>
  The exact rate limit (e.g., 5 stories per day, 10 per hour) depends on your deployment configuration. Check with your administrator for specific limits.
</Info>

Remove stories you no longer want:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the Story">
    Locate the story in your stories list.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Delete">
    Click the delete button (typically a trash icon).
  </Step>

## Limitations and Considerations

### Technical Limitations

- **Generation time**: Stories take 10-30 seconds to generate
- **Rate limits**: Limited number of generations per time period
- **AI variability**: Each generation is unique; regenerating produces different results
- **Length accuracy**: Word counts are approximate targets

### Content Considerations

- **AI interpretation**: Stories are AI-generated interpretations, not Scripture
- **Theological review**: Always compare with actual Bible text and trusted commentaries
- **Creative license**: Settings and perspectives are creative, not authoritative
- **Educational use**: Best used as a supplementary study tool, not primary source

<Note>
  AI-generated stories are creative tools for engagement and perspective-taking. They do not replace careful Bible study, prayer, and sound theological teaching.
</Note>
