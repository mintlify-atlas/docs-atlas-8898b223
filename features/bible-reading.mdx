The Bible Reading feature provides a modern, user-friendly interface for reading Scripture across multiple translations with seamless navigation and verse highlighting.

## Multiple Bible Translations

Access various Bible versions powered by API.Bible integration:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a Translation">
    Click on the Bible translation dropdown at the top of the reading interface. A searchable list of available translations will appear.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Search or Browse">
    Type to search for a specific translation (e.g., "KJV", "NIV", "ESV") or scroll through the available options.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Switch Translations">
    Select your preferred translation. The current chapter will automatically reload in the selected version.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Your selected translation is preserved in the URL, making it easy to bookmark or share specific passages in your preferred version.
</Info>

## Chapter Navigation

Navigate through the Bible with intuitive controls:

### Using Navigation Arrows

The chapter navigation provides left and right arrow buttons:

- **Left Arrow**: Navigate to the previous chapter
- **Right Arrow**: Navigate to the next chapter

Navigation arrows automatically disable when you reach the beginning or end of the Bible.

### Book and Chapter Selection

Beyond arrow navigation, you can:

1. **Jump to any book** using the book selector
2. **Select a specific chapter** from the chapter list
3. **Use URL parameters** to link directly to any passage

<Note>
  Chapter navigation respects the structure of your selected Bible translation, as different versions may organize books and chapters differently.
</Note>

## Verse Highlighting

Highlight specific verses for focused study:

### Automatic Highlighting

When you:
- Click a verse reference from search results
- Navigate from a collection
- Use a URL with a verse parameter (e.g., `?verse=JHN.3.16`)

The page will automatically:
1. Scroll to the verse
2. Highlight it for easy identification
3. Keep it in focus on the screen

### Linking to Verses

Share or bookmark specific verses by using verse references in your URL. The format follows the pattern:

```
/bible?bible=BIBLE_ID&chapter=CHAPTER_ID&verse=VERSE_ID
```

<Card title="Example" icon="link">
  Navigate to John 3:16 in the King James Version:
  `/bible?bible=de4e12af7f28f599-02&chapter=JHN.3&verse=JHN.3.16`
</Card>

## Reading Experience

### Desktop Experience

On desktop devices, the Bible reading interface offers:

- **Wide reading area** for comfortable extended reading sessions
- **Side-by-side notes panel** to view your personal notes alongside the Scripture
- **Persistent navigation controls** that remain accessible while scrolling
- **Clear verse formatting** with verse numbers for easy reference

### Mobile Experience

The mobile interface is optimized for smaller screens:

- **Full-width text** for maximum readability
- **Touch-friendly controls** for translation and chapter selection
- **Swipe-friendly navigation** with responsive arrow buttons
- **Sheet-based notes** that appear as a slide-up panel to maximize reading space

<Info>
  The reading interface automatically adapts to your device size, providing the best experience whether you're on a phone, tablet, or desktop computer.
</Info>

## Adding Verses to Collections

While reading, you can save verses to your personal collections:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign In">
    Ensure you're signed in to your account to access collections.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select a Verse">
    Click the add button next to any verse you want to save.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Collection">
    Select which collection to add the verse to, or create a new collection.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Learn more about organizing your saved verses in the [Collections](/features/collections) guide.

## Taking Notes

Enhance your Bible study by taking chapter-specific notes:

- On **desktop**: Click "Open Notes" to reveal a side-by-side notes panel
- On **mobile**: Use the notes sheet for a full-screen writing experience

For detailed information about the notes feature, see the [Notes](/features/notes) documentation.
